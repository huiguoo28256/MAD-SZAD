MAD-SZAD

MAD-SZAD is a privacy-compliant clinical multimodal dataset for schizophrenia and Alzheimer's disease analysis in real doctor-patient interview scenarios.

This dataset is introduced in the manuscript:
Causal-Guided Multimodal Diagnosis of Schizophrenia and Alzheimer’s Disease with MADD-CG and MAD-SZAD

Overview

Schizophrenia (SZ) and Alzheimer's disease (AD) may exhibit overlapping negative symptoms such as blunted affect, alogia, and social withdrawal, which can increase the risk of misdiagnosis in clinical practice. MAD-SZAD is designed to support research on robust and interpretable multimodal psychiatric diagnosis in real clinical interview settings.

The dataset includes audio-video samples from clinically confirmed SZ and AD patients, together with structured PANSS-based symptom information for causal and interpretable modeling.

Supported Tasks

  • Schizophrenia vs. Alzheimer's disease classification
  • Multimodal diagnosis under missing modalities
  • Question-slot-level incomplete input analysis
  • Causal-graph-guided multimodal modeling
  • Interpretable psychiatric diagnostic research
